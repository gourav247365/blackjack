How To Play?

Objective: Get a hand value closer to 21 than the dealer without exceeding 21.

Card Values: Number cards are worth their face value, face cards (J, Q, K) are 10 points, and Aces can be 1 or 11 points.

Initial Deal: Both player and dealer receive two cards. The player's cards are face up, while one of the dealer's cards is face down.

Player's Turn: Choose to "Hit" (take another card) or "Stand" (keep current hand). You can keep hitting until you decide to stand or your hand value exceeds 21.

Dealer's Turn: The dealer reveals their face-down card and must draw cards until their hand value is at least 17.

Winning: You win if your hand value is closer to 21 than the dealer's without exceeding 21. If your hand value exceeds 21, you bust and lose.

Blackjack: If your initial hand is an Ace and a 10-point card, you have a Blackjack and automatically win, unless the dealer also has a Blackjack, resulting in a tie.

Push: If both you and the dealer have the same hand value, it's a tie, and you neither win nor lose.

Restart: Start a new game with a fresh deck and new initial deal.

Tips: Remember, the key is to get as close to 21 as possible without going over, and to consider the dealer's visible card when deciding to hit or stand.
